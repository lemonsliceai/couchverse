Privacy Policy — Watch with Fox

Last updated: April 22, 2026

Watch with Fox is a Chrome extension that listens to the YouTube video playing in your active tab and uses AI to deliver real-time comedic commentary in the side panel. This page describes what data the extension handles and how it is used.

What we collect from your browser

  • Audio from the active YouTube tab. When you start a session, the extension captures the audio of the YouTube tab using Chrome's tabCapture API and streams it to our server in real time so the AI can hear what is playing. Tab audio is processed live and is not recorded, saved, or retained on our servers after the session ends.
  • The current YouTube video's URL and title. Used to give the AI context (so it can reference the video by name) and to detect when you navigate to a new video.
  • Playback state events. Play, pause, seek, and current playhead position, so commentary stays in sync with what you are watching.

The extension does not access your microphone, your camera, your location, your browsing history, or any other tabs.

What we store on your device

The extension stores your local preferences (commentary frequency, commentary length, volume levels, and an optional API URL override) in chrome.storage.local. Nothing is synced across devices and no personal information is stored.

What we store on our servers

For each session, our server may persist:

  • An anonymous session ID (UUID), the YouTube video URL, the video title, and timestamps.
  • Transcripts of the video audio and of the AI's commentary, used to maintain conversational context.

We do not collect or store names, email addresses, IP addresses for profiling, account credentials, payment information, browsing history outside the active YouTube session, or any other personally identifying information. Sessions are not linked to a user identity.

Third-party services

To generate commentary, the server forwards the minimum data required to the following providers:

  • LiveKit — real-time audio/video transport between the extension and the AI agent.
  • Groq — speech-to-text on the tab audio and language-model generation of commentary.
  • ElevenLabs — text-to-speech for the AI's voice.
  • LemonSlice — animated avatar rendering driven by the synthesized voice.

Each provider receives only what it needs to perform its function. We do not sell user data, and we do not share data with third parties for advertising, analytics, or any purpose unrelated to generating commentary.

Permissions

The extension requests the following Chrome permissions, all used solely to provide the commentary feature on YouTube:

  • tabCapture — capture the YouTube tab's audio.
  • activeTab — read the URL and title of the YouTube video.
  • scripting — inject the play/pause/seek monitor into youtube.com pages.
  • sidePanel — render the avatar and controls in Chrome's side panel.
  • storage — save your local preferences.
  • tabs — detect tab and URL changes so the session stays in sync.
  • Host access scoped to https://*.youtube.com/*.

Data retention

Tab audio and microphone audio are processed in real time and are not retained after the session ends. Anonymous session metadata and transcripts may be retained for service improvement and debugging. We delete server-side session records on request — see contact below.

Children

Watch with Fox is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

If we materially change how data is handled, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, surface a notice in the extension.
